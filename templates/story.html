MY STORY

30 Years in the Trades. Then Everything Changed.

I spent 30 years working with my hands. Landscaping, roofing, HVAC, plumbing, paving, steel fabrication — if it involved a job site, I was on it. I know what it's like to run a crew in January. I know what it's like to track jobs on paper and lose invoices in the truck.

During winter off-seasons, I started teaching myself to code. Not from a CS degree. Not from a bootcamp. From curiosity and stubbornness. I realized every trades business I'd ever worked for was running on spreadsheets, sticky notes, and "I'll remember that" — and I knew I could build something better.

My first client project was a steel fabrication shop — I built an 8-container Docker dashboard system. Real software, solving real problems, for a real business. That project proved I could build production systems for the trades industry.

Then I built 30 more systems. Lead generation. Email automation. Trades dashboards for six different industries. A testing platform with production readiness scoring. An institutional-grade automated trading platform — 219,000+ lines of Python, 830 files, built in about 4 weeks at 12-18 hours a day. All told, over 300,000 lines of production code across the entire ecosystem.

Now I'm building the business. Granite Models Automations isn't just a portfolio — it's an empire in progress. My mom handles marketing and day-to-day operations. I build. Two Dell OptiPlex servers on fiber are being set up to run everything. The goal is full business automation: SaaS subscriptions serving every trades company that needs this technology.

I'm currently employed at a landscaping company while building Granite Models on the side. This is the transition. Every system you see on this site was built in off-hours, weekends, and winters. That's the kind of commitment I bring to everything I do.
